History of Barkur

Barkur, once the capital of Tulu Nadu, is a town that boasts a rich history dating back to ancient times. It was once ruled by the Chola dynasty and later became an important center for the Vijayanagara Empire. Known for its temples, ruins, and ancient structures, Barkur provides a glimpse into the grandeur of South Indian culture and architecture.

Notable landmarks in Barkur include the Sri Bairagi Ganapathi Temple and other historical ruins scattered throughout the region. The town’s historical significance is further enhanced by its role as a center of learning and spirituality in its golden age.

Through centuries of rule, Barkur has developed a unique cultural and historical identity. Visitors can explore ancient stone temples, inscriptions, and architecture that reflect the artistry and devotion of the times. Barkur is not only a window into the past but also a vibrant part of Karnataka’s heritage.
